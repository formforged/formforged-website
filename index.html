What We Do

OUR
SERVICES

Two complementary manufacturing methods. One supplier. Simplified logistics for your project.

01

3D PRINTING

From rapid prototypes to functional end-use parts. We operate FDM, SLA, and SLS printers to match your material and tolerance requirements — with no minimum order quantity.

  • FDM · SLA · SLS Technologies
  • Layer resolution from 0.05mm
  • Build envelope up to 500×500×500mm
  • Engineering & flexible filaments
  • Post-processing & finishing
  • Colour matching available
02

PU FOAM MOULDING

Polyurethane foam cast parts for cushioning, gaskets, props, ergonomic grips, packaging inserts, and custom shapes. Small batch production using silicone or 3D-printed moulds.

  • Rigid, flexible & semi-rigid foams
  • Densities from 20 to 600 kg/m³
  • Silicone & 3D-printed tooling
  • Batch sizes from 1 to 500 units
  • Skin-effect & open-cell options
  • Insert moulding available
03

DFM REVIEW

Design for Manufacturability review included with every order. We analyse your files and flag potential issues before production begins, saving costly reprints.

  • File analysis: STL, STEP, OBJ, 3MF
  • Wall thickness & overhang checks
  • Mould draft angle optimisation
  • Tolerance recommendations
04

FINISHING & QC

Sanding, priming, painting, vapour smoothing, and dimensional inspection. Every part ships with a quality report including measured critical dimensions.

  • Acetone vapour smoothing (ABS)
  • Sanding grades 80–2000 grit
  • RAL / Pantone colour painting
  • Caliper & CMM inspection

We accept STL, STEP, OBJ, and 3MF files for 3D printing. For PU foam moulds, STEP or IGES is preferred for best accuracy. If you only have drawings (PDF or DXF), we can quote based on those and model the part for an additional fee.
FDM: ±0.2–0.5mm depending on geometry. SLA: ±0.1–0.2mm. SLS: ±0.3mm. PU foam casting: ±0.5–1.5mm depending on part complexity and foam density. For tight-tolerance features we recommend SLA resin or post-machining.
For simple parts, we 3D print the mould in-house, so tooling can be ready within 1–3 days. For complex geometries or high-volume runs requiring silicone moulds, allow 3–7 days for mould production before casting begins.
